Global Electricity Production

Electricity has transformed the world we live in. Ever since Thomas Edison created the world's first public power station in 1882 [1], production of electricity has not only increased, but also diversified in source. Whilst the first power station was powered by burning coal, electricity can now be generated through a large variety of sources, both renewable and non-renewable.

Unless otherwise specified, all electricity values are displayed in TeraWatt-Hours (TWh), which is 10^12 Watt-Hours. A Watt-Hour is a unit of measurement for electricity produced. One Watt-Hour of electricity is the equivalent to the total electricity produced by a one watt generator in one hour.

The chart on the right depicts how the each country has contributed to the world's cumulative electricity production per year. On average, the total amount of electricity produced has grown by approximately 2.9% annually from 1992 to 2019.

The lack of growth worldwide during the 2007-2009 period can be associated to the Global Financial Crisis, which began in 2007. During this period, due to a lack of financing [2], both supply and demand for electricity remained relatively stagnant, if not decreased.

In 2019, China produced the most electricity, with over 7,000 TWh of electricity being produced, followed by the United States of America, producing around 4100 TWh of electricity.
